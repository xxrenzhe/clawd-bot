## What is Moltbot (Openclaw)?

Before we dive into the "how-to," let’s clarify what we’re working with. **Moltbot** is an open-source, self-hosted AI gateway. Think of it as the "brain" that sits between your favorite messaging apps (Telegram, Discord, Slack) and the most powerful Large Language Models (LLMs) on the planet.

Unlike standard ChatGPT or Claude web interfaces, Moltbot offers:
- **Persistent Memory:** It remembers your brand voice and past research through file-based persistence.
- **Privacy:** Since you host it, your proprietary marketing strategies aren't being fed back into a public training loop.
- **Multi-Model Access:** You can swap between Claude (arguably the king of marketing copy) and local models via Ollama in a single conversation.

Whether you call it **Openclaw**, **Clawdbot**, or **Moltbot**, the goal is the same: total control over your AI agents.

## Setting Up Your Marketing Command Center

To get started, you need to install the gateway. If you’re worried about complex server configurations, don’t be. The installation is designed to be streamlined.

### 1. The Quick Install
Open your terminal and run the following command to get the core binaries on your system:

```bash
curl -fsSL https://clawd.bot/install.sh | bash
```

### 2. Onboarding your Assistant
Once installed, you need to configure your environment. This is where you’ll input your API keys for Claude or GPT-4.

```bash
clawdbot onboard
```

### 3. Verification
Before we start building marketing briefs, let's make sure everything is healthy:

```bash
clawdbot health
```

### 4. Launching the Gateway
To make Moltbot accessible to your messaging apps (like Telegram or Discord), launch the gateway:

```bash
clawdbot gateway --port 18789 --verbose
```

Congratulations! You now have a private AI gateway running. Now, let's make it work for your marketing team.

## Step-by-Step: Research Briefs and Content Outlines

Let’s look at a real-world scenario. Your marketing team publishes a weekly long-form article. Currently, it takes 4 hours just to find sources and organize an outline. We're going to cut that down to 15 minutes.

### Step 1: Install Openclaw and Define Your Sources
Once you have Moltbot running, you need to feed it the "truth." In your workspace folder, create a directory called `/sources`. 

Drop your brand voice guidelines, a list of trusted industry websites, and previous high-performing articles into this folder. Moltbot’s file-based memory will index these, ensuring that any brief it generates is grounded in your actual data.

### Step 2: Create a Marketing Brief Template
You don't want the AI to guess what a "brief" looks like. Create a Markdown file in your configuration called `brief-template.md`:

```markdown
# CONTENT BRIEF TEMPLATE
- **Target Audience:** [Who are we talking to?]
- **Core Angle:** [What is the unique hook?]
- **Primary Keyword:** [SEO Focus]
- **CTA:** [What should the reader do?]
- **Key Resources:** [Referenced from the /sources folder]
```

### Step 3: Run the Weekly Research Prompt
Connect Moltbot to Telegram or Discord (using the `clawdbot gateway` command we ran earlier). Now, you can simply message your bot:

*"Hey Moltbot, research the latest trends in [Topic] using my trusted sources list. Focus on angles that appeal to our 'Enterprise' persona. Summarize the top 3 findings."*

Because Moltbot is an AI gateway, it can reach out to various APIs or local models to synthesize this information without you ever leaving your chat app.

### Step 4: Generate the Draft Outline
Once you like the research findings, give the follow-up command:

*"Using the brief template and the research we just found, generate a detailed content outline. Include H2 and H3 headings and bullet points for what each section should cover."*

### Step 5: Review and Refine
At this stage, you have a 1,000-word research foundation. You can ask Moltbot to "play devil's advocate" or "find a more aggressive angle" for the introduction. Once satisfied, export the Markdown to your writing team.

## Security Considerations for Marketing Data

When you're dealing with unreleased product features or confidential marketing strategies, privacy is paramount. This is why self-hosting Openclaw is a game-changer compared to using "free" web-based wrappers.

To keep your marketing "war room" secure, follow these best practices:

1.  **Bind to Loopback:** In your configuration, ensure `gateway.bind` is set to `127.0.0.1` unless you are using a secure VPN or reverse proxy.
2.  **Use Pairing Mode:** Set your DM policy to "pairing." This means even if someone finds your bot's Telegram handle, they can't interact with it unless you manually approve their device.
3.  **Password Protection:** Always configure `gateway.auth.password` for an extra layer of defense.
4.  **Sandbox Tools:** If you use Moltbot in a group chat with external contractors, ensure your tools are sandboxed so they can't access your local file system.
